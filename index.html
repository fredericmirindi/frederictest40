Biography

With extensive experience in machine learning models for economic forecasting and policy analysis, he has pioneered innovative approaches to understanding economic phenomena through computational methods.

Teaching

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching approach combines theoretical foundations with practical applications, helping students understand complex economic concepts through real-world examples and computational methods.

Courses Taught

ECO110: Introduction to Microeconomics

Fundamental principles of microeconomic theory including supply and demand, market structures, consumer behavior, and firm decision-making. Students learn to analyze individual economic units and their interactions in various market environments.

MAT100: Applied Mathematics

Mathematical methods and applications in economics and business contexts. Coverage includes calculus, linear algebra, optimization techniques, and statistical foundations essential for economic modeling and analysis.

Research Methods

Comprehensive introduction to quantitative and qualitative research methodologies in economics and social sciences. Students learn data collection, analysis techniques, research design, and academic writing skills.

Business Information Systems

Fundamental concepts of information systems in business contexts, focusing on how technology supports organizational strategy, decision-making, and operations. Students explore system design, data management, and the role of IT in modern organizations.
